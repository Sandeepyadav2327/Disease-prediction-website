What is Typhoid Fever?

Typhoid fever is a bacterial infection caused by *Salmonella typhi*. It is a serious illness that affects the digestive system and is typically spread through contaminated food and water. Typhoid fever is more common in developing countries with poor sanitation.

Symptoms of Typhoid Fever

Common symptoms of typhoid fever include:

  • High fever
  • Weakness and fatigue
  • Abdominal pain
  • Loss of appetite
  • Diarrhea or constipation
  • Headache
  • Rash (rose-colored spots on the chest or abdomen)

Additional symptoms may include:

  • Enlarged liver or spleen
  • Confusion or delirium in severe cases

How to Prevent Typhoid Fever

  • Drink clean, boiled or bottled water.
  • Avoid eating raw or undercooked food, especially from street vendors.
  • Wash hands thoroughly after using the toilet and before eating.
  • Vaccination against typhoid fever is available and recommended in high-risk areas.

Treatment for Typhoid Fever

Typhoid fever is typically treated with antibiotics. Early treatment is important to avoid complications. In severe cases, hospitalization may be required. It is also essential to stay hydrated and rest during recovery.
