What is Dengue Fever?

Dengue fever is a viral illness caused by the dengue virus, transmitted primarily by Aedes mosquitoes. It is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions. Symptoms can range from mild to severe, and in some cases, dengue can progress to a more serious form known as severe dengue or dengue hemorrhagic fever.

Symptoms of Dengue Fever

Common symptoms of dengue fever include:

  • High fever (up to 104°F or 40°C)
  • Severe headache
  • Pain behind the eyes
  • Severe joint and muscle pain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Rash (can appear 3-4 days after the fever starts)

Additional symptoms may include:

  • Minor bleeding (nosebleeds or gum bleeding)
  • Low blood pressure
  • Enlarged lymph nodes

How to Prevent Dengue Fever

  • Eliminate mosquito breeding sites (remove standing water from containers).
  • Use mosquito nets or repellents, especially during early morning and evening.
  • Wear long-sleeved clothing to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Stay in well-screened or air-conditioned rooms.
  • Consider vaccination for dengue, available in some countries for high-risk populations.

Treatment for Dengue Fever

Currently, there is no specific antiviral treatment for dengue fever. Treatment focuses on relieving symptoms. This includes:

  • Staying hydrated by drinking fluids
  • Taking pain relievers like acetaminophen (avoid ibuprofen or aspirin)
  • Resting to allow the body to recover
  • In severe cases, hospitalization may be required to monitor blood pressure and fluid balance.
