What is Malaria?

Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by parasites that are transmitted to humans through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. It is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions. Malaria is preventable and treatable, but without proper care, it can lead to severe complications and even death.

Symptoms of Malaria

Common symptoms of malaria include:

  • High fever
  • Chills and sweating
  • Headache
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Muscle and joint pain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Abdominal pain

Additional symptoms may include:

  • Anemia (low red blood cell count)
  • Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)
  • Confusion or seizures in severe cases

How to Prevent Malaria

  • Use insect repellent with DEET on exposed skin.
  • Sleep under insecticide-treated mosquito nets, especially at night.
  • Wear long sleeves and pants to minimize mosquito bites.
  • Eliminate standing water around your home where mosquitoes breed.
  • Take antimalarial drugs as prescribed when traveling to endemic areas.

Treatment for Malaria

Malaria is treated with antimalarial medications. The treatment regimen depends on the type of malaria parasite, the severity of the disease, and the region where the infection was acquired. Some common treatments include:

  • Artemisinin-based combination therapies (ACTs) for uncomplicated malaria
  • Chloroquine or quinine for certain strains of malaria
  • Hospitalization and intravenous treatment for severe malaria cases
