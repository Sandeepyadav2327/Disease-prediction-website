Causes of Heart Disease

Heart disease can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, lifestyle choices, and existing medical conditions. Here are some common causes:

  • High Blood Pressure: Can damage the heart and blood vessels over time.
  • High Cholesterol: Leads to the buildup of fatty deposits in the arteries.
  • Smoking: Increases the risk of heart disease by damaging blood vessels.
  • Obesity: Being overweight puts extra strain on the heart.
  • Diabetes: Increases the risk of heart disease due to damage caused by high blood sugar levels.

Symptoms of Heart Disease

Common symptoms of heart disease may include:

  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
  • Irregular heartbeat
  • Swelling in the legs, ankles, or feet
