What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic medical condition where the body is unable to properly process food for use as energy. It occurs when the body doesn't produce enough insulin or doesn't use insulin effectively. Insulin is a hormone produced by the pancreas that allows cells to absorb glucose from the blood.

There are two main types of diabetes: Type 1 and Type 2. Type 1 is typically diagnosed in children and young adults, while Type 2 is more common in adults and is linked to lifestyle factors such as diet and exercise.

Symptoms of Diabetes

Common symptoms of diabetes include:

  • Increased thirst and frequent urination
  • Extreme fatigue and tiredness
  • Blurred vision
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Slow-healing sores or frequent infections
  • Increased hunger
  • Numbness or tingling in hands or feet

How to Prevent Diabetes

While Type 1 diabetes cannot be prevented, Type 2 diabetes is often preventable by making healthy lifestyle changes. Here are some tips:

  • Maintain a healthy weight.
  • Exercise regularly (at least 30 minutes a day).
  • Eat a balanced diet, rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.
  • Quit smoking and limit alcohol consumption.
  • Monitor your blood sugar levels regularly if at risk.
